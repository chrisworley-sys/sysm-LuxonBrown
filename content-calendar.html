Ongoing Optimization
Campaign Considerations

Based on early performance data from March, these are the key areas we'll be actively monitoring and optimizing as campaigns scale across all persona groups.

What We're Watching & Optimizing

Informed by March 2026 Facebook performance data

📈

Performance Monitoring

Track CTR, CPC, engagement rate, and link clicks per campaign. March data shows strong early signals — Pete & Pam at $0.60 CPC and Randy at 5.8% CTR. We'll establish per-persona benchmarks as new campaigns build data.

$0.60 CPC · Below industry avg
🎨

Introducing New Creatives

Refresh ad creative monthly alongside theme changes. Photography-led visuals for each new whitepaper topic keep campaigns from going stale and align messaging with the current lead magnet offer.

Monthly creative refresh per group
🔬

A/B Testing

Test headlines, imagery, and audience segments within each persona group. Run 2 ad variations per campaign to identify top performers, then consolidate spend behind the winner mid-month.

2 variations per campaign
💰

Scaling Ad Spend

March spend was $163.78 across 2 campaigns. With 4 campaigns now active and strong CPC performance, there's room to increase total monthly budget while maintaining efficient cost-per-lead. Ad frequency at 1.6 confirms no audience fatigue.

Frequency 1.6 · Room to scale
👥

Audience Refinement

March data shows women 65+ as the top-responding demographic across both active campaigns. We'll use this to sharpen targeting, build lookalike audiences, and adjust persona-level ad sets as more data comes in.

Women 65+ leading engagement
📱

Organic Social Growth

March organic reach was limited with only 2 posts (20 total views). Scaling to 4 posts per month per group with theme-aligned content will build page credibility and support paid campaigns when prospects visit the profile.

Increasing from 2 to 12+ posts/mo
