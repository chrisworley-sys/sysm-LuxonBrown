Ongoing Optimization
Campaign Considerations

We're still in the early stages — these campaigns just launched and the data will become more meaningful over time. That said, the initial signals are encouraging and give us a strong foundation to build on. Here's where we're focused as we scale.

Where We're Headed & What We're Building Toward

Early insights from March 2026 · Numbers will sharpen as campaigns mature

📈

Performance Monitoring

We're tracking CTR, CPC, engagement rate, and link clicks across every campaign. Even with limited data, the early reads are promising — Pete & Pam at $0.60 CPC and Randy at 5.8% CTR right out of the gate. As campaigns run longer, we'll establish reliable per-persona benchmarks to guide budget decisions.

$0.60 CPC · Strong early signal
🎨

Introducing New Creatives

Each month brings a new theme and whitepaper, which means fresh creative across every channel. Photography-led visuals tailored to each topic keep the campaigns feeling relevant and give us natural opportunities to test what resonates best with each persona group.

Fresh creative every month
🔬

A/B Testing

As we build a larger data set, we'll run 2 ad variations per campaign to test headlines, imagery, and audience segments. Once we identify clear winners, we consolidate spend behind the top performer — getting smarter with every cycle.

2 variations per campaign
💰

Scaling Ad Spend

March was a controlled ramp at $163.78 across 2 campaigns — intentionally modest while we dialed in targeting. With 4 campaigns now active and early CPCs well below financial services benchmarks, we're positioned to gradually increase budget with confidence. Ad frequency at 1.6 confirms plenty of headroom before audience fatigue.

Frequency 1.6 · Ready to grow
👥

Audience Refinement

Early engagement trends show women 65+ responding most strongly across both active campaigns — a great starting point. As data accumulates, we'll sharpen targeting, build lookalike audiences from top responders, and fine-tune persona-level ad sets to maximize relevance.

Women 65+ · Early front-runner
📱

Organic Social Growth

Organic is just getting started — March had only 2 posts as we prioritized paid launch. Scaling to 4 posts per month per group will build the page presence that makes paid campaigns more effective. When prospects click an ad and visit the profile, a consistent content feed builds immediate credibility.

Growing to 12+ posts/mo
💼

LinkedIn Ads Expansion

Facebook is proving the model — the next step is expanding to LinkedIn to reach Business Owner Betty and Pre-Retiree Paul where they're most professionally active. LinkedIn's targeting by job title, company size, and industry is tailor-made for these higher-AUM personas and will complement the Facebook campaigns already in market.

Betty + Paul · Professional targeting
⚡

Campaign Objective Mix

An interesting early signal: Randy's lead gen campaign posted a 6.5% engagement rate in just 3 days, outpacing Pete & Pam's conversion-optimized campaign. It's too early to draw conclusions, but we'll be watching whether lead gen objectives make a stronger launch strategy before shifting to conversions once pixel data matures.

6.5% engagement · Worth watching
🎯

Landing Pages & Conversion Tracking

With 246 paid link clicks already driving to whitepaper download pages, we want to make sure every click counts. As traffic grows, we'll focus on optimizing landing page load time, form length, and CTA placement — turning the strong top-of-funnel performance into measurable downloads and consultation bookings.

246 clicks · Maximizing every visit
