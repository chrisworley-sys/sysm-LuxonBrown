New Compliance Workflow

Current vs. proposed review timelines.

Today, all content goes through external Comply review regardless of risk level. The proposed two-tier system reserves external review for content that genuinely requires it — cutting turnaround in half for the majority of pieces and getting campaigns to market faster.

Fast Track
~2 weeks
For staff announcements, brand content, educational posts, and other low-risk material with no regulated claims.
1Content creation (SuperSymm) · 3–5 days
2Internal review · Compliance Hub (Taylor / Chris M) · 48 hours
3Approval & publication · 24 hours
Estimated ~75% of content

The impact: By routing low-risk content (staff posts, educational content, brand material) through internal review only, we accelerate roughly three-quarters of the content calendar from ~3 weeks to ~2 weeks. Higher-risk content still receives the full external review it requires. The result is a faster content calendar without compromising compliance integrity.

Key takeaways

The story of Q1 in seven points.

●

Since launching in March, paid campaigns have reached over 26,000 people and driven an estimated 883 link clicks at $0.94 per click — strong efficiency for financial services advertising.

●

The Pete & Pam life insurance ad remains the largest campaign with 18,050 people reached and 427 clicks at $0.88 per click.

●

The lead gen campaign expanded to four ads — Betty, Randy, Paul, Bob. Betty leads on click volume (177), Paul delivers the lowest CPC ($0.68), and Randy has an exceptional 5.8% CTR.

●

Spending is scaling as planned. April has already exceeded March's total spend ($429 vs. $404) in just 12 days, reflecting the expansion from two ads to five.

●

The top-responding audience is women 65+ across both campaigns, followed by men 65+ and women 55–64 — consistent with the demographic actively planning for retirement and tax strategy.

●

Ad frequency remains healthy at 1.64 across all campaigns. There's room to grow spend without overexposing the audience.

●

One thing to watch: The Bob ad is receiving very little spend from Meta's delivery system ($5.96 total). We'll evaluate whether to adjust targeting or creative in the coming weeks, or reallocate budget to the top performers.
