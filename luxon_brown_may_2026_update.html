Social Posting

Diversifying the social posting.

May's 5-posts-per-week pace dropped views/post by 54%. June drops to 3 posts/week and widens the mix of post types we cycle through — no single format every week, multiple ways into the same audience. Plus an active engagement layer that converts passive scrolling into direct outreach.

01
VSL clips
Short clips from the Brandon video — talking-head, on-message, 30–60 seconds.
Why: Faces build trust faster than text on cold audiences. Brandon's expertise comes through better in motion than in a static graphic.
02
Whitepaper & lead magnet promos
Direct-promo posts pointing to landing pages with downloadable guides (SS Taxation PDF, biz owner guide, 401k checklist).
Why: Captures identified leads straight from social. The SS Taxation PDF alone generated 4 of 9 May leads — there's clear demand to monetize.
03
Team highlights & culture
Posts featuring Chrissy, Gavin, Linda, Brandon, Taylor — quotes, day-in-the-life, milestones.
Why: Wealth management is a relationship business. Audiences buy from people they recognize, and the team needs faces on the feed.
04
"Schedule a call" direct CTA
Single-purpose posts that skip education and go straight to the booking link.
Why: Not every viewer needs to be nurtured — some are ready right now. A direct CTA surfaces them.
05
Educational posts
Stat-driven, hook-led content on the active monthly theme (SS optimization, retirement tax, 401k rollover).
Why: Still the engagement backbone — 19–27% interaction rates in May. The proven path needs to stay in the mix.
06
Active engagement (DMs)
Engaged followers — likers, commenters, savers — get a relevant DM with a direct LP link (VSL, whitepaper, or scheduler).
Why: Closes the loop on the warmest signal. Passive scroll → personalized DM → identified lead.

Phase 2 — Deepen the funnel

Mid · Weeks 3–6

Goal: Build the assets that strengthen Phase 1 sequences and open up new lead segments — both prospects and existing clients.

Brandon video landing page (hub)
A single polished destination for the Brandon video that runs throughout every Phase 1 sequence. Lifts perceived professionalism and gives every email, post, and DM a place to land.
Referral + annual review campaign
A 3-email sequence triggered around each client's annual review. Existing clients are the firm's best lead source — this surfaces referrals at the moment trust is highest, and reinforces relationship health.
Cold lead re-engagement
A wake-up sequence for Wealthbox contacts dormant 6+ months. Zero acquisition cost, surfaces buyers who weren't ready before but are now. The existing list is an underused asset.
Polished client email templates
Standardize the onboarding, cross-sell, and annual-review emails currently varying by advisor. Templates raise quality, save advisor time, reinforce the brand, and become the backbone of Funnel C.

Phase 3 — Scale

Later · Weeks 7–12

Goal: Convert proven sequences into self-serve tools and brand assets that scale beyond one-to-one advisor touches — so growth doesn't require linearly more advisor time.

Retirement calculator deploy
A self-serve calculator drives top-of-funnel engagement without advisor time. POC already exists; needs Brandon validation pass and finalized UX before deploy. Doubles as a lead capture mechanism.
Retirement roadmap wizard
Digitize the existing LB workbook into an interactive lead magnet. Captures qualified leads while educating prospects on what working with LB actually looks like — pre-frames the sales conversation.
Podcast pilot (2–3 episodes)
Long-form audio builds trust at depth — and gives LB shareable assets for social, email, and DMs. Pilot first to test format and reception before committing to a full season.
Fall seminar system ready
Q4 seminar prep starts in Phase 3 so the full system — registration LPs, email sequences, retargeting flows from Phase 1 — is live and ready when fall seminars run.
