Key Takeaways

●

Since launching in March, your paid campaigns have reached over 26,000 people and driven an estimated 883 link clicks at an average cost of $0.94 per click — strong efficiency for financial services advertising.

●

The Pete & Pam life insurance ad (running since Mar 1) remains the largest campaign with 18,050 people reached and 427 clicks at $0.88 per click.

●

The lead gen campaign has expanded to four ads — Betty, Randy, Paul, and Bob — each targeting a different audience. Betty (tax optimization) is the top performer at 177 clicks, and Paul (tax readiness) is delivering the lowest CPC at $0.68.

●

Spending is scaling up as planned. April has already exceeded March's total spend ($429 vs. $404) in just 12 days, reflecting the expansion from two ads to five. Reach and click volume are growing alongside it.

●

Your top-responding audience continues to be women age 65+ across both campaigns, followed by men 65+ and women 55–64. This is consistent with the demographic most actively planning for retirement and tax strategy.

●

Ad frequency remains healthy at 1.64 across all campaigns — no signs of ad fatigue. There's still room to grow spend without overexposing your audience.

●

On the organic side, four posts were published in March — all life insurance content supporting the Pete & Pam campaign. Organic reach is small (91 people across all posts), but trending upward. The Mar 28 post was the strongest performer, generating the only organic link clicks of the period.

●

Organic posting builds page credibility when prospects from paid ads visit your profile. Increasing posting frequency and expanding topics beyond life insurance in April will strengthen the page and support the new tax and retirement campaigns.

●

One thing to watch: The Bob (retiring business owner) ad is receiving very little spend from Meta's delivery system ($5.96 total). This likely means the audience pool is small or the ad isn't winning in Meta's auction. We'll evaluate whether to adjust targeting or creative on this ad in the coming weeks.
